Expand What happens next after I sign up for Transfer Funds?

You’ll need to verify your email address and add your accounts with other banks or credit unions.

  • We’ll send you a welcome email with a verification code. On the Preferences page, select the Verify link next to your email address. Enter the verification code and Submit.

  • To add your accounts with other banks or credit unions, select the Add a New Account link on the Transfer Funds page, or select the + Add Account button on the Preferences page under My Other Accounts.

Expand Why do I have to verify my accounts in Transfer Funds?

We do everything possible to ensure your security. The verification procedures are safeguards that help us make sure that only you can add accounts from other banks or credit unions to your service.

Expand Can I make changes to my Transfer Funds account?

Absolutely! You can easily add or delete your accounts with other banks or credit unions on the Preferences page.

Expand How often will I have to verify my accounts in Transfer Funds?

For your protection, we will ask you to verify each account that you add.

Expand Which accounts are eligible in Transfer Funds?

Your MyFinancial Bank - 88851113 accounts are already enabled for this service.

You can also add these types of accounts from other banks or credit unions:

  • Checking and savings accounts that accept ACH transfers. Check with your bank or MyFinancial Bank - 88851113 if you’re not sure. You can also add checking or savings accounts that belong to other people.

  • Business accounts that you own, or for which you are an authorized user.

  • Investment accounts that you own, if your brokerage is displayed in the Brokerage Account menu. To see the brokerages listed, select Brokerage for Account Type.

You’ll need to verify accounts before you can use them.

Expand Which account types are not eligible in Transfer Funds?

You won’t be able to add these accounts to your service:

  • Individual Retirement Accounts (IRAs).

  • Custodial and/or Trust Accounts.

  • Certificates of Deposits (CDs) or other time-based accounts.

  • Loan accounts (including credit card and equity accounts).

You may have other accounts that are ineligible for this service because of restrictions from your other banks or credit unions.

Expand How do I add the accounts I have with other banks or credit unions in Transfer Funds?

Select Add new account from the Transfer Funds page and select the type of account you want to add, or select the + Add Account on the Preferences page.

  • For checking, Savings, or money market accounts, enter the routing number and account number.

  • For investment accounts, select Brokerage from the Account Type menu. Select a supported firm from the Brokerage Account menu and enter the requested account information.

Verify Your Account

For security, you must verify each account you add before you can use it for transfers.

  • For online verification, you’ll be asked to provide your login credentials for the account you are adding. Online verification is quick and completely safe, and we never store your credentials.

  • If online verification is not available or if it fails, you can use the trial deposit verification, which takes 2-3 business days.

Verified accounts in good standing show as Active on the Preferences page.

Expand What is an ABA routing number, and how do I find it?

The American Bankers Association (ABA) routing number identifies the bank. You can find this nine-digit number at the bottom of your checks or deposit slips between the symbols.

Expand How do I find the account number?

Your account number appears at the bottom of your checks between the symbols. You can also find your account number on your bank statement.

Expand I’m an existing customer. How do I add additional accounts in Transfer Funds?

You can add new accounts the same way you’ve added them before.

Expand How is a brokerage account defined in Transfer Funds?

A brokerage account is defined as the cash account (usually a Money Market account) that you hold at the brokerage to buy or sell stocks or other investments.

Expand Which brokerage accounts are eligible in Transfer Funds?

Most of the nation's prominent brokerage firms can be used in Transfer Funds. Select Brokerage under Account Type to review the firms available in the Brokerage Account menu.

Expand Can I add a brokerage account that belongs to someone else in Transfer Funds?

No. Only brokerage accounts you own may be added to the service.

Expand How do I add a brokerage account in Transfer Funds?

Select Add a New Account on the Transfer Funds, or select + Add Account on the Preferences page.

  1. Select Brokerage as the Account Type.

  2. Select a brokerage firm from the Brokerage Account menu and enter the requested information. If your account includes check-writing privileges, you will be asked for the bank routing number.

  3. Verify your account.

    • Select Send me two deposits to start the trial deposit process.

    • In 2-3 business days, find the amounts we deposited in the brokerage account.

    • Enter the amount of each deposit by selecting the Verify link at the top of the Transfer Funds page or the Verify link on the Preferences page.

Expand What if my brokerage firm isn’t listed in Transfer Funds?

Unfortunately, if your brokerage firm isn’t in the menu, you won’t be able to add an account with that firm to your service.

Expand Can I add accounts that belong to other people in Transfer Funds?

Yes. You may add checking or savings accounts that belong to other people to the service. The account owner must provide you with his or her account details and email address.

You can only transfer money into, not out of, accounts that belong to other people.

Expand How to I add accounts that belong to other people in Transfer Funds?

There are two ways to add an account that belongs to someone else.

  • On the Transfer Funds page, select the transfer To someone else option. Select the Add a Recipient’s Bank Account link and add the requested information.

  • On the Preferences page, select the + Add a Person’s Account button and add the requested information.

The newly added account will appear on the Preferences page under Other People’s Accounts. After you verify it, the will be available in the To account menu when you transfer To someone else.

Expand Which business accounts are eligible in Transfer Funds?

You can add business checking or money market accounts if you have check-writing privileges for those accounts. You must own the account, or be an authorized user. You can also add business savings accounts.

As with all accounts, any limits or conditions that apply to your business account also apply to the transfers made through this service.

Expand When is instant account verification possible?

We can instantly verify accounts if the account’s bank or credit union is networked with the third party verification service we use.

Expand What do I need to do for instant account verification? Is it safe?

We’ll ask you to enter your sign in credentials for the account you are adding. This process is completely safe, and takes less than a minute. We do not store your sign in credentials.

Verified accounts are immediately available for transfers. If the verification was unsuccessful, we’ll ask you to verify your account using a different method.

Expand When will I get trial deposits to verify my accounts?

If your account is not instantly verified, we’ll ask you to start the trial deposit verification process. MyFinancial Bank - 88851113 will make two small deposits into the account you are adding.

  • In 2-3 days, look online for the small (less than $1) deposits, or call your bank or credit union.

  • On the Preferences page, select the Verify link next to your account. You’ll be prompted to enter the two deposit amounts. After you enter the correct amounts, your account will be active and ready for transfers.

Expand How do I find the trial deposit amounts?

You should see the two small deposits (less than $1) in your account online 2-3 days after starting the verification process. Or, you can call your bank or credit union or review your monthly statement.

Expand What do I do if the trial deposits don’t show up in my account?

We’ll email you if we can’t make the two small deposits to your account. The account you are trying to add will show as Failed or Not Verified on the Preferences page.

If this happens, we suggest you delete the account because it can’t be used for transfers. You can add it again with the correct information to start the trial deposit process again.

Expand How can I tell which accounts still have to be verified in Transfer Funds?

Your unverified accounts will have a Pending Verification status on the Preferences page.

Be sure to verify your accounts so you can use them to transfer money.

Expand What is the definition of a bank account for the purposes of this service?

Checking accounts, money market accounts for which you have check-writing privileges (other than those held through brokerages) and savings accounts are eligible for the Transfer Funds as bank accounts. You can add accounts held at credit unions by selecting Add Bank Account link.

Expand What is the definition of a Third Party bank account for the purposes of this Service?

Checking Accounts,moneymarket accounts for which someone other than you have check-writing privileges (other than those held through brokerages) and savings accounts are eligible for the Transfer Funds as third party bank accounts. You can add accounts held at credit unions by selecting Add Bank Account link.

Expand How do I add Third Party bank accounts to my Accounts Portfolio?

Clicking Add Accounts from the menu bar and you will be taken directly to the Add Bank Accounts page. Select the "Someone else is the owner of the account I'm adding" radio button and then search for the financial institution either by name or by 9-digit ABA/Routing Number.


Step 1--Entering the institution name

Enter the name of the financial institution in the data entry field. For best results, please enter a significant portion of the institution name. Note that typing the full institution name may prevent a successful search if our database doesn't include the name exactly as you've entered it. Finally, do not use abbreviations.

Once you perform the search, you'll see hyperlinks for one or more institutions matching your search data. Click on the link for the institution you want.

Alternatively, you can search for the institution by entering the 9-digit ABA Routing number usually found at the bottom of a check on the left hand side.

Step 1-Providing account Information

You'll be brought to a page asking for the name of the account owner, account number and ABA routing number of the account. Enter the first name, last name and account number in the data fields and select the proper ABA routing number from the drop-down list. See below for information about ABA Routing/Transit numbers, including what they are and how to find them.

When you have supplied all requested information, click Submit.

  • You will be brought to the the Account Enabled for Funds Transfer page confirming that this account is now ready to be used with the Transfer Funds Service. The account will also be listed under the Third party Account Section on the Preferences page.

Expand How are transfers to other people’s accounts in Transfer Funds different from Popmoney?

Transfer Funds uses Popmoney for transfers made to other people’s accounts, which is helpful if you don’t have another person’s bank account information.

If you use Popmoney:

  • The accounts you add for other people in Transfer Funds are also available in Popmoney. However, the activity for the transfer will appear only in the service that sent it.

  • The limits and fees for transferring money to other people’s accounts are the same as your limits and fees in Popmoney.

Expand What is a one-time transfer?

A one-time transfer occurs only once.

Expand How do I set up a one-time transfer?

On the Transfer Funds page, enter the transfer amount and select the From and To accounts. Use the Send date displayed or select a new date. The Frequency needs to be One Time Transfer.

On the review page you can Confirm, Edit, or Cancel your transfer.

After confirming, you can select the Print icon to print the details.

Expand What are my delivery options?

Your delivery options are displayed on the Transfer Funds page after you enter an amount and select a From and To account. The delivery options show the completed transfer date and fees, if any.

Expand Can I set up a transfer today to process later?

Yes, you can schedule transfers up to one year in advance.

Expand What is a scheduled transfer?

A scheduled transfer is a future-dated transfer.

Expand Can I edit a transfer?

Yes. Select the Edit link next to a pending transfer on the Activity page and save your changes. You can also edit recurring transfers this way.

You can’t edit transfers that don’t have an Edit link.

Expand How do I check the status of my transfers?

Each transfer has a status on the Activity page. Select the information icon infoinfo next to the word Status in the top row of the table to see the definitions for each status.

Expand How will I know when a transfer is complete?

We’ll email you when a transfer is complete. We’ll also display your transfers for the past 180 days on the Activity page.

As always, you can check your account statement to verify that the transfer was debited or credited as you requested.

Expand What is a recurring transfer?

A recurring transfer repeats for the same amount each time, at the frequency and duration that you specify.

Expand How do I set up recurring transfers?

On the Transfer Funds page, enter the amount and select the From and To accounts. You’ll need to change today’s date to a future date.

From the Frequency menu, choose how often you want the transfers to be sent.

From the Duration menu, choose when you want the transfers to stop. Your choices are:

  • Until I cancel.

  • Up to a specified amount (be sure to provide the dollar amount in the field that displays under Duration).

  • Up to a specified number of transfers (be sure to provide the total number of transfers in the field that displays under Duration).

  • Until a specified date (be sure to provide the end date in the field that displays under Duration).

Select Review. After reviewing, you can Confirm, Edit, or Cancel your recurring transfer.

After confirming, you can select the Print icon to print the details.

Expand What are my frequency options for recurring transfers?

You have these options in the Frequency menu for recurring transfers.

Once every month Every month on the same date selected for the first send-on date.
Every 15 days On the same date as the first transfer plus 15 days. (i.e.: If the first transfer send-on date is 4/5, the next transfer dates will be 4/20, 5/5, and 5/20.
Every week Every week on the same day of the week selected for the first transfer.
Every 2 weeks Every other week on the same day of the week selected for the first transfer.
Every 2 months Every other month on the same send-on date as the first transfer.
Every 3 months Every three months on the same send-on date as the first transfer.
Every 6 months Every six months on the same send-on date as the first transfer.
Every year Once a year on the same send on date as the first transfer.
Expand Can I change the number of remaining transfers in a recurring plan?

No. You can change the amount and date of the recurring transfers, but you won’t be able to change the number of transfers in the plan.

You may want to cancel the recurring transfer plan and set up a new recurring plan with the number of transfers needed.

Expand What are my transfer limits?

You can transfer up to your assigned dollar limits.

Expand Do the limits and/or fees from my accounts at other banks or credit unions apply in Transfer Funds?

Yes. The transaction limits and/or fees used for your accounts with other banks and credit unions apply to the transfers you make in Transfer Funds.

We recommend that you ask your bank or credit union if any restrictions or fees apply when using those accounts with your service.

Expand How are limits calculated for the 30-day period in Transfer Funds?

When you schedule an immediate transfer, we calculate the amount of the transfers you’ve made within the past 30 days. Your remaining limit is the difference between your 30-day limit and the amount you’ve transferred in the past 30 days from today.

When you schedule a future-dated transfer, we calculate the limit on the send date of the transfer. If the remaining limit is less than the transfer amount, we’ll cancel the transfer and send you an email.

Expand How much does it cost to transfer funds?

Faster delivery speeds may be offered for a service fee. Fees, if any, will be displayed next to the delivery date.

You can view more information by selecting the View Fees and Delivery link above the delivery options.

Expand What are the cutoff times for canceling today’s transfers?

Cutoff times are listed in the Fees and Delivery Info link on the Transfer Funds page. You’ll need to select a From and To account to see your delivery options and the link.

You can cancel today’s transfers from the Activity page before the cutoff time.

  • Future-dated transfers can be canceled before the Send date.

  • You can’t cancel a transfer that’s in process. If you create a new transfer to move the money back, please check to see if you’ll be charged fees.

Expand How do I cancel a transfer?

Find the transfer on the Activity page and expand it to see the details and Cancel button. For recurring transfers, you’ll be given the option to cancel just the next recurring transfer, or all the remaining transfers in the plan.

Expand Can I stop a transfer sent to someone else’s account after the send date?

Yes, you can stop a transfer to someone else if the transfer is still pending on the Activity page. Expand the transfer to view the details and stop option.

Expand What can make my transfer unsuccessful?

A transfer will be unsuccessful (returned) if it can’t be withdrawn from or deposited to an account. We’ll email you if a transfer is unsuccessful, and the details will be available on the Activity page.

Common reasons for unsuccessful transfers include:

  • Insufficient funds in the From account.

  • Exceeding the limit for a transfer amount.

Expand How do I get details about an unsuccessful transfer?

On the Activity page, select the Rejected link to see the details about your unsuccessful transfer.

Expand What do the different status designations for Recurring Transfers mean?

Pending: There are Recurring Transfers remaining.


Complete: All the Recurring Transfers have been executed.


Cancelled: User has cancelled the Recurring Transfers.

Expand How do I cancel a Recurring Transfer?

Find the transfer on the Activity page and expand it to see the details and Cancel Transfer button. You’ll be given the option to cancel all the remaining transfers in the plan, or just cancel the next one.

Expand How do you provide security and protect my privacy?

We are committed to maintaining the privacy, security and integrity of the financial and personal information you share with us, and take every precaution to do so. We have designed multiple levels of security to ensure the protection of your information from loss, theft, unauthorized access, modification or disclosure. We make sure that our security measures conform to all applicable federal, state and local laws.

  • Our procedures also include extensive in-house privacy measures. Your user login name, password, and all verification information pertaining to your individual accounts, always remain encrypted. Neither our software, nor any of our employees can decode this information. Please see our Privacy Policy for more details.
